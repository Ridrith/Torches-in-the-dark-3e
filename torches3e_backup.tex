\section{Introduction}
\begin{center}
  \MaybeGraphic[height=1.2cm]{demon-head.png}
\end{center}
\bloodrule

You aren't a hero, you're a knave, a scoundrel, a rogue. A survivor. Play your cards right, and you might stay that way. \textit{Torches in the Dark} is a grim, dark fantasy tabletop role-playing game where survival is a struggle, and death lurks in every shadow.

%==================================================
\section{Campaign Primer}
\bloodrule

\subsection{Pillars of the Setting}

\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{summarybox}[title={\textsc{The Gods Are Silent}}]
The gods are gone—and they will not return. There is no divine magic present in \textit{Torches in the Dark}. The prime pantheon is entirely absent, or simply uncaring. Mortals are left to the mercy of the terrible things that lie in the dark between the stars.
\end{summarybox}

\columnbreak

\begin{grimnote}[title={\textsc{In the Wake of a Fallen Empire}}]
You explore in the shadows of an old empire. The God Binder ruled for nearly a thousand years before being slain by the last line of rebellious kings. Ancient armies and things long forgotten now roam freely.
\end{grimnote}
\end{multicols}

\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{grimnote}[title={\textsc{The Wytch Spires}}]
The Wytch Spires—once horrific prisons where the God Binder's legions convened—now "abandoned," draw even darker things to their lightless halls.
\end{grimnote}

\columnbreak

\begin{warningbox}[title={\textsc{The Enemy in the Dark}}]
When the sun goes down, dread begins. The living are not alone; the border between light and shadow is thin and easily crossed.
\end{warningbox}
\end{multicols}

\subsection{The World of Ohr}

\textit{Torches in the Dark} and the World of Ohr are inspired by pulp greats—Robert E. Howard, Fritz Leiber, Clark Ashton Smith, Michael Moorcock, and Charles R. Saunders—mixing sword \& sorcery with gothic and high-fantasy elements.

\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Brutal Combat:} Fast and bloody. Magical healing is rare to nonexistent beyond rest and scarce herbs.
\item \textbf{Magic is Dangerous:} Uncommon and corrupting, tainted by cosmic horrors beyond the veil.
\item \textbf{Larger-Than-Life:} PCs are beings of will—spend coin, feast, gamble, and burn bright.
\item \textbf{Not Heroes:} Motivated by self-interest: coin, thrill, leverage.
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\subsection{Locations in Ohr}

\begin{multicols}{2}
{\footnotesize
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Iron Principalities} — Mercenary lords, city-kings at war; devils and demons lurk at night.
\item \textbf{The Red Sands} — Ocileb, the city of the emperor; salacious rumors, harlot goddess, sex cults.
\item \textbf{The Numb} — Brutal cannibal tribes, barbarian warriors, advancing glaciers.
\item \textbf{Old Andminas} — The kingdom of tombs; pyreborn, undead, fallen knights.
\item \textbf{Riva Della Morte} — Ancient jungles, primordial reptiles, serpent tombs.
\item \textbf{Empire of Swords} — Inquisitors, wytch burnings, peasant armies.
\item \textbf{Dahnari Desert} — Dragon cults, fire wurms, devil-men raiders.
\item \textbf{The Walking Wilds} — Misty jungles, savage picts, Komuh homeland.
\item \textbf{Idruin} — Wastelands and salt flats, quakes and volcanoes.
\item \textbf{Ainerth} — Shrine city to the God Binder; zealotry, crusades.
\item \textbf{Ixindar} — Steaming jungles; serpent lords, naga, lizardmen.

\begin{grimnote}[title={\textsc{A Broken World}}]
These sites are a sampler. Monsters are rare, often unique, and hide in forgotten places. Slavery is common; serfs are slaves in all but name. Racism and prejudice are endemic. Bring a character with a spine and a story—or don't come.
\end{grimnote}

\subsection{Myth of Creation}
\begin{multicols}{2}
{\footnotesize
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Keeper (Light):} First; wove fate and stars; spent herself seeding the void.
\item \textbf{Kor'nu (Time):} Spoke divine syllables; matter formed; time unborn.
\item \textbf{Ein Sof (Thought):} Lit stars, molded worlds, framed laws; found \textbf{Shaldrot (Shadow)}.
\item \textbf{Shaldrot:} Blind, chaotic; birthed abominations; could not be destroyed.
\item \textbf{The Sefirot (Unity):} Named and empowered; built realms, the vault of mirrors.
\item \textbf{War in the Void:} Shaldrot devoured worlds; Ein Sof slew it; cast antithetical shadows.
\end{itemize}
}
\end{multicols}

\subsection{Ability Scores}
Characters begin with 10 attribute points to distribute among nine ability scores. No ability score may exceed +6 at character creation.

\begin{summarybox}
\begin{itemize}
  \item 10 Starting Points
  \item +6 Max at Start
  \item 9 Abilities
\end{itemize}
\end{summarybox}

\noindent\textbf{Example Distribution:} Might +2, Finesse +2, Endurance +1, Insight +1, Resolve +0, Presence +2, Faith +0, Magick +0, Luck +2.

\subsection{Races}
Some races are rare. To play a rare race, roll a d100 and roll equal to or lower than the number shown. Playing as a non-human race \textbf{is} your \term{Grim Sort}.

\begin{multicols}{2}
{\small
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Human} \;|\; Common — +1 to any ability score, choose one passive and active trait.
\item \textbf{Empire Dwarf} \;|\; 40\% — +3 Endurance, +2 Might, \term{Iron Gut}.
\item \textbf{Drakon} \;|\; 20\% — +2 Might, +3 Faith, \term{Unshakable Faith}.
\item \textbf{Orkon} \;|\; 20\% — +4 Might, +1 Endurance, \term{Tenacious}.
\item \textbf{Astral Elf} \;|\; 10\% — +2 Magick, +3 Presence, \term{Void Gaze}, \term{Quiet Mind}.
\end{itemize}
}
\end{multicols}

\section{Core Mechanics}
\bloodrule

\subsection{The Strikes System}
Every character begins with a number of strikes equal to their class's strike count. For every hit taken, a character loses 1 strike worth of damage. Powerful enemies may cause 2 or 3 strikes of damage.

\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Defense} = Finesse + 5 + Armor Bonus
\item \textbf{Base Save DC} = 15
\item \textbf{Checks} = d20 + relevant Ability
\end{itemize}

\columnbreak

\subsection{Equipment \& Inventory}
Players can hold $10 + \text{Endurance}$ slots of equipment.
\end{multicols}

\begin{compacttabular}{@{} l c c p{.45\linewidth} @{}}
\toprule
\rowcolor{thead}
\textbf{Armor} & \textbf{Slots} & \textbf{DEF} & \textbf{DR Range (d6)} \\
\midrule
Light Armor  & 1 & +4 & 1/6 (roll 1) \\
Medium Armor & 2 & +2 & 2/6 (roll 1--2) \\
Heavy Armor  & 3 & -2 & 3/6 (roll 1--3) \\
\bottomrule
\end{compacttabular}

\noindent\textit{Damage Reduction:} When hit, you may roll a d6; if the result is within your armor's range, reduce the hit by 1 Strike.

\subsubsection{Shields \& Helmets}
\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Shield:} +1 to DR chance; destroy to negate one physical attack.
\item \textbf{Helmet:} +1 Defense; critical becomes normal hit, then destroyed.
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\subsection{Resting \& Healing}
\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Sanctuary Rest:} Regain all strikes in 24 hours at sanctuary.
\item \textbf{Breather:} Heal 1d4 strikes once per day during short rest.
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\subsection{Combat Mechanics}
\subsubsection{Initiative}
\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Group-Based:} GM rolls d6: 1--3 enemies first, 4--6 players first.
\item \textbf{Single:} Roll d20 + Insight; highest acts first.
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\subsubsection{Critical Hits \& Effects}
\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Natural 20:} +1 Strike of damage.
\item \textbf{Natural 1:} GM determines horrible outcome.
\item \textbf{Weapon Effects:} Knockdown, bleeding, sundering.
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\bloodrule

\begin{multicols}{2}
\subsection{Spellcasting (Arcane Magic)}
Wytches roll 1d20 + Magick vs the spell's DC. \textbf{Base Spell DC: 10.}

\subsubsection{Armor Interference}
\begin{itemize}
\item Light Armor: +4 to spell DC
\item Medium Armor: +8 to spell DC
\item Heavy Armor: +12 to spell DC
\end{itemize}

\columnbreak

\subsection{Miracles (Divine Magic)}
Crusaders, Prelates, Pyromancers channel divine power. Gods ignore mortals. \textbf{Base Miracle DC: 12.}

\subsubsection{Divine Mishaps (Natural 1)}
On a natural 1: (1) Lose 1 Faith; (2) Roll 1d100 on Divine Mishap Table.

\section{The Omen Called Night}
\bloodrule
\textit{When the last rays of Aith'na sink and the sky bruises to black, the Omen called Night wakes and walks.}

\subsection{Trigger}
The Dread system is active whenever all present light is gone, smothered, or too weak to reach beyond arm's length, or the place itself is lightless.

\subsection{Darkness Level}
Track DL from 0 to 6. Each minute in exploration or each round in combat without adequate light increases DL by +1.

\subsection{Light and DL}
\textbf{Dim light} holds DL steady. \textbf{Normal light} reduces DL by 1 per round to a minimum of 0. \textbf{Bright bonfire or daylight} resets DL to 0 and suppresses Dread effects for 10 minutes.

\subsection{Last Light}
If the final light dies, advance to DL 6 at once. The Dread acts before the first player turn.

\subsection{Dread Effects by Darkness Level}
\textit{Each level brings the night closer, and the night has teeth}

\begin{tabular}{@{}p{1cm}p{3cm}p{9cm}@{}}

\midrule
1 & Unease & All Resolve saves are at disadvantage until light returns. \\
2 & Disorientation & Insight and Finesse checks at disadvantage. You drift unless you declare physical contact. \\
3 & Shadows Lengthen & Each character makes a Resolve save DC 12 or suffers Morale Broken. \\
4 & Grasping Dark & One random character is attacked by unseen claws. Fang and Claw damage. Ignores DR. \\

\textbf{Attack:} Dust Cloud. Endurance DC 13 or blinded and poisoned for 1d4 turns.

\textbf{Special:} Whispers implant false memories. Resolve DC 15 or accept a harmful lie.

\textbf{Weakness:} Fragile. Fire forces morale checks.

\subsection{Running the Dread}
\textit{How to make the darkness matter}

Do not stall. Advance DL when light falters or the scene demands it. Let your players feel the clock tick.

Use short sentences and hard cuts. Let table results interrupt plans.

When in doubt, add breath, cold, and sound. The Dread is a sensory predator.

Reward redundancy. The fool with one torch dies first. The bastard with three lives long enough to be unlucky later.

If a result would be dull where they stand, move the group or move the horror. The Dread wants them. It will come.

You aren't a hero, you're a knave, a scoundrel, a rogue. A survivor. Play your cards right, and you might stay that way. \textit{Torches in the Dark} is a grim, dark fantasy tabletop role-playing game where survival is a struggle, and death lurks in every shadow.

\section{Core Mechanics}
\bloodrule

\subsection{The Strikes System}
Every character begins with a number of strikes equal to their class's strike count. For every hit taken, a character loses 1 strike worth of damage. Powerful enemies may cause 2 or 3 strikes of damage.

\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Defense} = Finesse + 5 + Armor Bonus
\item \textbf{Base Save DC} = 15
\item \textbf{Checks} = d20 + relevant Ability
\end{itemize}

\columnbreak

\subsection{Equipment \& Inventory}
Players can hold $10 + \text{Endurance}$ slots of equipment.
\end{multicols}

\begin{compacttabular}{@{} l c c p{.45\linewidth} @{}}
\toprule
\rowcolor{thead}
\textbf{Armor} & \textbf{Slots} & \textbf{DEF} & \textbf{DR Range (d6)} \\
\midrule
Light Armor  & 1 & +4 & 1/6 (roll 1) \\
Medium Armor & 2 & +2 & 2/6 (roll 1--2) \\
Heavy Armor  & 3 & -2 & 3/6 (roll 1--3) \\
\bottomrule
\end{compacttabular}

\noindent\textit{Damage Reduction:} When hit, you may roll a d6; if the result is within your armor's range, reduce the hit by 1 Strike.

\subsubsection{Shields \& Helmets}
\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Shield:} +1 to DR chance; destroy to negate one physical attack.
\item \textbf{Helmet:} +1 Defense; critical becomes normal hit, then destroyed.
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\subsection{Resting \& Healing}
\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Sanctuary Rest:} Regain all strikes in 24 hours at sanctuary.
\item \textbf{Breather:} Heal 1d4 strikes once per day during short rest.
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\subsection{Combat Mechanics}
\subsubsection{Initiative}
\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Group-Based:} GM rolls d6: 1--3 enemies first, 4--6 players first.
\item \textbf{Single:} Roll d20 + Insight; highest acts first.
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\subsubsection{Critical Hits \& Effects}
\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Natural 20:} +1 Strike of damage.
\item \textbf{Natural 1:} GM determines horrible outcome.
\item \textbf{Weapon Effects:} Knockdown, bleeding, sundering.
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\section{The Omen Called Night}
\bloodrule
\textit{When the last rays of Aith'na sink and the sky bruises to black, the Omen called Night wakes and walks.}

\subsection{Trigger}
The Dread system is active whenever all present light is gone, smothered, or too weak to reach beyond arm's length, or the place itself is lightless.

\subsection{Darkness Level}
Track DL from 0 to 6. Each minute in exploration or each round in combat without adequate light increases DL by +1.

\subsection{Light and DL}
\textbf{Dim light} holds DL steady. \textbf{Normal light} reduces DL by 1 per round to a minimum of 0. \textbf{Bright bonfire or daylight} resets DL to 0 and suppresses Dread effects for 10 minutes.

\subsection{Last Light}
If the final light dies, advance to DL 6 at once. The Dread acts before the first player turn.

\subsection{Dread Effects by Darkness Level}
\textit{Each level brings the night closer, and the night has teeth}

\begin{tabular}{@{}p{1cm}p{3cm}p{9cm}@{}}
\textbf{DL} & \textbf{Name} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
1 & Unease & All Resolve saves are at disadvantage until light returns. \\
2 & Disorientation & Insight and Finesse checks at disadvantage. You drift unless you declare physical contact. \\
3 & Shadows Lengthen & Each character makes a Resolve save DC 12 or suffers Morale Broken. \\
4 & Grasping Dark & One random character is attacked by unseen claws. Fang and Claw damage. Ignores DR. \\
5 & Dragged Screaming & One random character makes an Endurance save DC 14 or is pulled into black and separated. \\
6 & The Hunt & Each round, 1d4 shadow beasts attack until light is restored. Their strikes ignore DR and cause Bleeding Wound. \\
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Running the Dread}
\textit{How to make the darkness matter}

Do not stall. Advance DL when light falters or the scene demands it. Let your players feel the clock tick.

Use short sentences and hard cuts. Let table results interrupt plans.

When in doubt, add breath, cold, and sound. The Dread is a sensory predator.

Reward redundancy. The fool with one torch dies first. The bastard with three lives long enough to be unlucky later.

If a result would be dull where they stand, move the group or move the horror. The Dread wants them. It will come.
